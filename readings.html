Recommended Readings

A curated selection of books related to psychoanalysis, depth psychology, and the exploration of unconscious life. These are not easy reads—they require patience, thought, and engagement. But they offer something rare: genuine insight into the complexities of human experience.

Foundational Psychoanalytic Texts

[Book Cover]

The Interpretation of Dreams

Sigmund Freud

Freud's foundational work on the unconscious, dream symbolism, and the royal road to understanding mental life. Essential reading for anyone interested in psychoanalysis.

View Book
[Book Cover]

The Language of Psychoanalysis

Jean Laplanche & Jean-Bertrand Pontalis

A comprehensive dictionary of psychoanalytic concepts. Invaluable for understanding the precise meanings of key terms in psychoanalytic thinking.

View Book
[Book Cover]

Papers on Technique

Sigmund Freud

Freud's clinical papers outlining the fundamentals of psychoanalytic practice, including transference, resistance, and the analytic relationship.

View Book

Contemporary Psychoanalytic Thought

[Book Cover]

Why Psychoanalysis?

Elisabeth Roudinesco

A defense of psychoanalysis in the contemporary world, addressing its critics and explaining its ongoing relevance for understanding human subjectivity.

View Book
[Book Cover]

The Trauma of Everyday Life

Mark Epstein

An exploration of trauma through a psychoanalytic and Buddhist lens, examining how we live with and work through ordinary suffering.

View Book

On Sexuality and Desire

[Book Cover]

Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality

Sigmund Freud

Freud's revolutionary work on infantile sexuality, perversion, and the development of sexual life—central to psychoanalytic thinking.

View Book
