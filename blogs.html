Symptoms are not malfunctions to be eliminated. In psychoanalysis, they are understood as meaningful expressions—communications from the unconscious about conflicts, desires, and experiences that have not been fully thought through or put into words.

There is a cultural expectation that therapy should produce rapid results. Psychoanalysis resists this expectation. Not because it is inefficient, but because the work it undertakes—exploring the unconscious, working through conflicts, developing self-awareness—cannot be rushed.
