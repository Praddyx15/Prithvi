Psychoanalytically Speaking

A psychoanalytic approach to mental health and inner life

This is a space for individuals seeking long-term, depth-oriented psychological work. Psychoanalysis is not about quick fixes or symptom removal—it is an exploration of your inner world, your history, your relationships, and the patterns that shape your life.

The work here is grounded in psychoanalytic tradition: thoughtful, contained, and serious. It requires time, continuity, and a willingness to think deeply about yourself. There are no promises of rapid change, no behavioral techniques, no diagnostic labels imposed upon you.

What This Practice Offers

A psychoanalytic space to explore the unconscious dimensions of your experience—your childhood, your sexuality, your relationships, your symptoms as meaningful expressions rather than problems to be eliminated.

This practice does not offer CBT, DBT, REBT, EMDR, hypnotherapy, or psychological testing. It offers a relationship in which you can begin to hear yourself, notice patterns, and become aware of what has been operating beneath the surface of your conscious life.

Change, when it happens, is slow, deep, and structural—not a matter of behavioral modification, but of understanding. The stance here is one of curiosity, not judgment; listening, not advising; exploration, not prescription.

Understanding the Mind

Psychoanalysis explores the inner landscape of your mind, tracing patterns back to their origins through careful, sustained inquiry.

Time & Patience

Real change requires time. This is not a quick fix, but a gradual unfolding of understanding through depth-oriented work.

A Note on Beginning

If you are considering psychoanalytic work, take your time. Read through the materials here, think about what resonates, and reach out when you feel ready. There is no urgency, no pressure. This is a decision that deserves careful thought.
